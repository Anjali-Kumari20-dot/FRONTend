Photography : Where art meets reality

  • The First Photograph

    In 1826, Joseph Nicéphore Niépce took the first permanent photograph, "View from the Window at Le Gras." This image captured a moment in time, freezing the beauty of the French countryside.

  • Capturing the Human Experience

    Photographers like Lewis Hine and Jacob Riis used their cameras to expose social injustices and bring attention to the struggles of the working class and immigrants.

  • Preserving Memories

    During World War II, photographers like Robert Capa and Gerda Taro risked their lives to capture the reality of war. Their images serve as a reminder of the devastating consequences of conflict.

  • Iconic Images

    Images like Alfred Eisenstaedt's "V-J Day in Times Square" (1945) and Nick Ut's "Napalm Girl" (1972) have become ingrained in our collective memory, symbolizing moments of joy, tragedy, and resilience.

  • The Power of Photography

    Photography has the ability to evoke emotions, spark empathy, and inspire change. It has been used to raise awareness about social and environmental issues, and to celebrate the beauty and diversity of human experience.
